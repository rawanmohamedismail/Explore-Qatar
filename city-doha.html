Overview

The Heart of Qatar

Doha is one of the most dynamic cities in the world — a place where glass skyscrapers rise behind a 7-kilometre waterfront corniche once walked by pearl divers, where world-class museums stand minutes from a 500-year-old souq, and where residents from over 150 nationalities work and live together in one of the Gulf's most cosmopolitan capitals.

The city grew remarkably fast. In the early 20th century, Doha was a modest fishing and pearl-diving settlement of a few thousand people. Today it is a city of over 1.2 million — the political, cultural, economic and sporting capital of Qatar, home to Hamad International Airport (one of the world's best), the iconic Museum of Islamic Art, and the National Museum of Qatar, shaped like a desert rose.

Doha hosted the 2022 FIFA World Cup, receiving over 1.4 million visitors and cementing its status as a truly global destination. Its West Bay skyline — a forest of mirrored towers reflected in the Gulf — has become one of the most recognisable city silhouettes in the Arab world.

Did You Know?

Doha's name in Arabic — 'الدوحة' — means 'the big tree' or 'the rounded bay', referring to the bay upon which the original settlement was built. The city's first municipality was established in 1963.

Top Attractions in Doha

Culture

Museum of Islamic Art

Designed by I.M. Pei, this iconic waterfront museum houses 1,400 years of Islamic art and artefacts. One of the world's great museums and a Doha landmark.

Heritage

Souq Waqif

Doha's ancient market — a labyrinth of alleyways selling spices, falcons, traditional garments, and handicrafts. The best place to experience old Qatar.

Beach

The Corniche

A 7-kilometre waterfront promenade framing the West Bay skyline — perfect for sunset walks, cycling, and dhow spotting. The soul of Doha.

Culture

The Pearl-Qatar

A man-made island of luxury marinas, international restaurants, boutique shopping and stylish residential towers — Qatar's answer to Monaco.

Heritage

National Museum of Qatar

Jean Nouvel's spectacular desert-rose building tells the story of Qatar from ancient times to the present. A must-visit architectural wonder.

Culture

Katara Cultural Village

A stunning cultural hub with amphitheatres, galleries, restaurants, mosques, and a beautiful beach — the artistic heart of Doha.

Getting to Doha

By Car

Doha is at the centre of Qatar's road network. Most international hotels are within 15–20 minutes of Hamad International Airport via the Expressway.

By Metro

The Doha Metro's Red, Green, and Gold lines connect the airport, West Bay, Souq Waqif, Education City, and Al Wakrah. Single tickets from QAR 2.

By Taxi

Karwa taxis (light blue with maroon roof) are metered and widely available. Uber and Careem also operate. Initial fare QAR 4 + QAR 1.20/km.
