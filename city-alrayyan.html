Overview

Qatar's Hub of Learning and Sport

Al Rayyan is Qatar's second most populous municipality, stretching west and northwest of Doha and home to over 600,000 residents. It is arguably the most important municipality in Qatar outside the capital — containing Education City, Aspire Zone Sports City, the Mathaf Arab Museum of Modern Art, Mall of Qatar, and two FIFA World Cup stadiums.

Education City is Al Rayyan's crown jewel — a vast Qatar Foundation campus hosting branch campuses of eight world-leading universities including Carnegie Mellon, Georgetown, Northwestern, Virginia Commonwealth, Texas A&M, Cornell Medicine, HEC Paris, and University College London. It is also home to the Qatar National Library, the Qatar Science and Technology Park, and a stunning mosque designed by I.M. Pei.

Aspire Zone — also known as Sports City — is a 250-hectare sports complex built for the 2006 Asian Games, housing Khalifa International Stadium (upgraded for the 2022 World Cup), Aspire Academy (Qatar's elite sports school), Hamad Aquatic Centre, and vast parks. It is one of the most complete sports campuses in the world.

Did You Know?

Education City's mosque — officially the Imam Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab Mosque — was designed by I.M. Pei, the same architect behind the Museum of Islamic Art. With a capacity of 1,800 worshippers, it is considered one of his most beautiful buildings.

Top Attractions in Al Rayyan

Culture

Education City

Qatar Foundation's flagship campus — eight world-class university branches, the Qatar National Library, Science & Technology Park and the stunning Education City Mosque on a beautiful green campus.

Sport

Aspire Zone — Sports City

A 250-hectare world-class sports complex with Khalifa International Stadium, Aspire Academy, Hamad Aquatic Centre, and vast parks. One of the world's finest sports campuses.

Culture

Mathaf: Arab Museum of Modern Art

The Arab world's premier museum of modern art — housing over 9,000 works by Arab artists, with rotating international exhibitions in a landmark heritage building.

Sport

Ahmad Bin Ali Stadium

A 44,740-seat stadium that hosted 2022 FIFA World Cup matches — its façade features intricate geometric patterns inspired by Qatari culture and identity.

Culture

Mall of Qatar

One of Qatar's largest malls — 500+ stores, a Snow Dunes snow park, a 20-screen cinema, and a vast food court. A complete family entertainment destination.

Nature

Aspire Park

Qatar's largest public park — 88 hectares of green lawns, jogging paths, a boating lake, outdoor gyms, and picnic spots. Free, open 24 hours, and very popular with families.

Getting to Al Rayyan

By Car

Al Rayyan is 10–20 km west of central Doha. Education City and Aspire Zone are well-signposted and connected by the D-Ring Road and Al Rayyan Road.

By Metro

The Doha Metro Green Line serves Education City Station (for Qatar Foundation) and the Red Line connects to Aspire Zone. Fast, comfortable and affordable.

By Bus

Qatar Transit's bus network serves Al Rayyan extensively, including routes to Education City, Mall of Qatar, and Aspire Zone from central Doha.
