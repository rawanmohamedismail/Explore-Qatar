Overview

Where Ancient Heritage Meets Natural Beauty

Al Khor — meaning 'the creek' in Arabic — is one of Qatar's oldest cities, founded by the Al Muhannadi tribe in the mid-18th century on the banks of a natural coastal creek on Qatar's northeastern shore. For generations it was a thriving centre of fishing and pearl diving, before oil revenues transformed it into a modern city of 36,000 residents working primarily in Qatar's oil and gas industry.

Today, Al Khor is celebrated as one of Qatar's finest nature destinations. The Al Thakhira Mangrove Reserve — Qatar's oldest and largest — stretches along the coast just north of the city, offering extraordinary kayaking through winding waterways where flamingos, herons, crabs, and fish abound. Nearby Purple Island (Jazirat Bin Ghannam) is a magical spot for kayaking, camping, and birdwatching, particularly during winter months when migratory birds arrive in spectacular numbers.

Dominating the city's skyline is Al Bayt Stadium — a breathtaking 60,000-seat arena shaped like a traditional Bedouin tent (bayt al sha'ar), which hosted the opening match of the 2022 FIFA World Cup. The stadium is surrounded by Al Bayt Stadium Park — 30 football pitches' worth of open green space with cycling tracks, play areas, basketball and tennis courts.

Did You Know?

Al Khor is named after the Arabic word for 'creek' or 'bay'. When a freshwater source was discovered near the coast in the 19th century, the Al Muhannadi tribe built Ain Hleetan Well — believed by locals to have medicinal properties — and constructed three cylindrical watchtowers to defend it from pirates.

Top Attractions in Al Khor

Nature

Al Thakhira Mangroves

Qatar's largest mangrove reserve — paddle a kayak through enchanting waterways lined with mangrove trees. Spot flamingos, herons, crabs and fish in one of the Gulf's most beautiful ecosystems.

Sport

Al Bayt Stadium

The breathtaking 60,000-seat arena shaped like a Bedouin tent that hosted the opening match of the 2022 FIFA World Cup. A landmark of architectural brilliance.

Nature

Purple Island

A small island named for the ancient purple-dye industry that operated here in the 2nd millennium BCE. Today ideal for kayaking, camping, and winter birdwatching.

Heritage

Al Khor Watchtowers

Three restored cylindrical watchtowers that once guarded the city's precious freshwater well from pirates. A striking heritage landmark on the Corniche.

Culture

Al Khor Archaeological Museum

A fascinating regional museum on the Corniche exploring traditional fishing, pearl diving, and shipbuilding. Contains geological maps showing Qatar's coastline changes over 120,000 years.

Beach

Al Farkiah Beach

A beautiful, family-friendly beach of golden sand and turquoise water — fenced for safety with a children's play area, pergolas, and barbecue spots.

Getting to Al Khor

By Car

Take the Lusail Expressway north from Doha, then continue on Al Shamal Road. Journey is approximately 60 km and takes 45 minutes under normal traffic conditions.

By Metro + Bus

Take the Doha Metro Red Line to Lusail QNB, then board a regional bus service to Al Khor City from Lusail Bus Station. Total journey around 75 minutes.

By Taxi

Karwa taxis and ride-hailing apps (Uber, Careem) serve the route. A taxi from central Doha costs approximately QAR 80–120 one way.
