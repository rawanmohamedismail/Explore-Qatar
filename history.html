History

The History of Qatar

A journey through millennia of settlement, trade, pearl diving, and remarkable transformation.

Since 50,000 BCContinuous Human Settlement
Ancient Roots

A Land of Ancient Civilisations

Archaeological evidence suggests that Qatar has been inhabited for at least 50,000 years. Finds at Al Wusail and other sites reveal the presence of Stone Age communities who exploited the rich coastal resources of the Gulf.

The peninsula's position along ancient trade routes made it an important node for commerce between Mesopotamia and the Indus Valley civilisations. Qatar's shores were known to early Greek geographers, and references to the peninsula appear in the works of Ptolemy.

Key Periods

Qatar Through the Ages

Each era of Qatar's history has left an indelible mark on its culture, architecture, and identity.

Maritime

The Pearl Diving Era

For centuries, Qatar's economy was built on pearl diving. Qatari pearls were prized across the world, and the industry shaped the country's social structure and architecture.

Modern

Discovery of Oil

The discovery of oil in 1939 and subsequent exploitation of vast natural gas reserves transformed Qatar from a modest pearl-diving state into one of the world's wealthiest nations.

Independence

Independence in 1971

Qatar gained full independence on 3 September 1971, establishing itself as a sovereign state and beginning a remarkable journey of nation-building.
