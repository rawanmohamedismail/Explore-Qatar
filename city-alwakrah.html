Overview

Qatar's Most Authentic Town

Al Wakrah is exactly what you might imagine when picturing a traditional Arabian coastal town. Despite its proximity to Doha — just 25 km south of the capital — it has retained a character entirely its own, offering a genuine escape from the modern city's soaring skyscrapers into a world of traditional architecture, fish markets, and ancient maritime heritage.

For centuries Al Wakrah was a humble fishing and pearl-diving village, and the rhythms of the sea still shape daily life here. The beautifully restored Al Wakrah Souq — a maze of traditional alleyways and whitewashed courtyard buildings — is one of the finest heritage souqs in Qatar, selling spices, handicrafts, fresh fish, and traditional goods. Nearby stands a restored 17th-century mosque and the historic Al Wakrah Fort.

Modern Al Wakrah is also home to Al Janoub Stadium — designed by the late Dame Zaha Hadid, with a sinuous sail-like form inspired by traditional Qatari dhow boats — which hosted 2022 FIFA World Cup matches. To the south of Al Wakrah, the road leads to Mesaieed, gateway to the Inland Sea (Khor Al Adaid) and Sealine Beach — one of Qatar's finest dune landscapes.

Did You Know?

Al Janoub Stadium was the last major project completed by the legendary architect Dame Zaha Hadid before her death in 2016. Its form — inspired by the dhow boats of Qatar's pearl-diving heritage — is considered one of her finest works.

Top Attractions in Al Wakrah

Heritage

Al Wakrah Souq

A beautifully restored traditional souq — wander alleyways of whitewashed buildings selling spices, textiles, handicrafts and fresh seafood. One of the most authentic market experiences in Qatar.

Sport

Al Janoub Stadium

Zaha Hadid's masterpiece — 40,000 seats under a dhow-sail-inspired roof that opens and closes. Hosted 2022 FIFA World Cup group matches. A stunning architectural icon.

Heritage

Al Wakrah Old Mosque

A beautifully preserved 17th-century mosque at the heart of the heritage district — one of Qatar's oldest surviving mosques and a centre of the community for centuries.

Heritage

Al Wakrah Heritage Village

A collection of restored traditional Qatari houses, wind towers, and pearl-diving equipment that recreates the atmosphere of old coastal Qatar. A living museum of maritime life.

Beach

Al Wakrah Beach & Corniche

A clean, family-friendly beach with calm Gulf waters, a pleasant corniche for walking, and traditional cafés where fishermen still bring in their morning catch.

Nature

Mesaieed & Inland Sea Gateway

Just south of Al Wakrah, Mesaieed is the gateway to Qatar's most spectacular natural wonder — Khor Al Adaid (the Inland Sea), where towering dunes plunge into the sea.

Getting to Al Wakrah

By Car

Al Wakrah is 25 km south of Doha via the Doha Expressway (Highway 1). The drive takes approximately 20–25 minutes. Well-signposted from central Doha.

By Metro

The Doha Metro Gold Line runs to Al Wakrah Station — making it one of the easiest out-of-Doha destinations to reach by public transport. Journey from central Doha around 30 minutes.

By Taxi

Karwa taxis and Uber/Careem serve Al Wakrah regularly. A taxi from central Doha costs approximately QAR 40–60 one way.
