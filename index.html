About Qatar

A Land of Extraordinary Contrasts

Qatar is a small peninsula on the northeastern coast of the Arabian Peninsula, jutting into the Persian Gulf. Despite its size, this remarkable nation captivates visitors with a unique blend of ancient Bedouin heritage and futuristic architecture.

From the gleaming towers of West Bay to the tranquil dunes of the Inland Sea, Qatar offers experiences that linger long in the memory. The nation has transformed itself into one of the world's most ambitious destinations.

  • World-class museums including the iconic Museum of Islamic Art
  • Authentic souq experiences in Souq Waqif
  • Year-round sunshine and pristine Arabian Gulf beaches
  • Rich Bedouin traditions and warm hospitality

Capital City

Doha: City of Wonders

Doha, the capital and largest city of Qatar, is a dazzling metropolis of striking contrasts. The city's iconic skyline, dominated by the Burj Doha and the distinctive buildings of the West Bay, is best experienced from the waterfront Corniche.

Beyond the gleaming towers lies a city of extraordinary cultural depth — from the labyrinthine alleys of Souq Waqif to the serene Museum of Islamic Art park, Doha rewards every curious traveller.
