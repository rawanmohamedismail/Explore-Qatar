The Capital

Doha: A City Like No Other

Doha, the capital and most populous city of Qatar, is one of the fastest-growing cities in the world. Its skyline — dominated by bold architectural statements along the West Bay Lagoon — has become one of the most recognisable in the Middle East.

Yet Doha is far more than its gleaming towers. The historic Souq Waqif, the Corniche waterfront promenade, and the extraordinary Museum of Islamic Art all offer layers of experience that reward repeated exploration.

Must-See

Doha's Top Attractions

Museum of Islamic Art

One of the world's finest collections of Islamic art and artefacts, housed in an iconic I.M. Pei-designed building on Doha's Corniche.

Souq Waqif

Doha's beloved traditional market, restored to its original mud-plastered form, filled with spices, handicrafts, falcons, and traditional restaurants.

The Corniche

A stunning 7km waterfront promenade offering breathtaking views of Doha's modern skyline across the bay.

The Pearl-Qatar

An artificial island offering luxurious living, high-end retail, fine dining, and a glamorous marina lined with superyachts.

Katara Cultural Village

Qatar's premier cultural hub hosting theatre performances, art exhibitions, film festivals, and a beautiful amphitheatre.

Aspire Zone

A world-class sporting complex featuring the Khalifa International Stadium, the Aspire Dome, and extensive public parks.

Explore Everything Doha Has to Offer

From museums to markets, Doha is a city that never stops surprising its visitors.
