Overview

Where Time Stands Still

Al Zubarah — located on Qatar's remote northwestern coast — is one of the most extraordinary heritage sites in the entire Arabian Peninsula. The preserved ruins of a once-thriving 18th-century pearl-trading town were inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2013, recognising their outstanding universal value as a record of the pearling economy that shaped this entire region for centuries.

Founded around 1762, Al Zubarah rapidly grew into one of the most prosperous trading ports on the Gulf — a city of 6,000 to 10,000 people at its peak, exporting pearls to India, Persia, and Europe, and importing goods from across the world. The town was protected by a substantial defensive wall, watchtowers, and a deep coastal canal allowing dhows to dock safely. It was abandoned around 1811 following a military attack, and the desert slowly reclaimed its streets — preserving them in extraordinary condition beneath the sands.

Today, visitors can walk the excavated streets of old Al Zubarah, exploring the remains of courtyard houses, the souq, mosques, and the distinctive canal system. The spectacular Al Zubarah Fort — a 1938 Qatari fort standing sentinel over the ruins — houses an excellent museum interpreting the site's history. The surrounding landscape of flat desert, with the ruins stretching to the sea, is hauntingly beautiful and entirely unlike anywhere else in Qatar.

Did You Know?

Al Zubarah was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in June 2013 — making it Qatar's first and only UNESCO World Heritage Site. Excavations by archaeologists from UCL, Copenhagen, and QM have revealed the town's remarkably well-preserved street plan, houses, and defensive structures beneath the desert surface.

Top Attractions in Al Zubarah

Heritage

Al Zubarah Archaeological Site

Walk the excavated streets of an 18th-century pearling city — courtyard houses, a marketplace, mosques, watchtowers and the remarkable coastal canal that once docked trading dhows from across the world.

Heritage

Al Zubarah Fort

A majestic 1938 Qatari fort with four distinctive round towers — standing guard over the ruins and housing a compelling museum about the site's history, excavations, and the pearling era.

Nature

Coastal Wetlands & Birds

The flat coastal plains around Al Zubarah attract remarkable birdlife, especially migratory species between October and March. A popular birdwatching destination in Qatar's remote north.

Beach

Al Ghariya Beach

A pristine, uncrowded beach of white sand and crystal-clear water near Al Zubarah — one of the most beautiful and peaceful beaches in Qatar, rarely busy due to the remote location.

Heritage

Al Jumail Ghost Village

On the road to Al Zubarah, the abandoned village of Al Jumail is a hauntingly beautiful collection of empty stone and coral houses, a ruined mosque, and a crumbling boat. The perfect complement to the main site.

Heritage

Al Ruwais — Qatar's Oldest Mosque

Al Ruwais, the nearest inhabited town, contains a 17th-century mosque believed to be the oldest in Qatar — a remarkable historic mosque still in active use.

Getting to Al Zubarah

By Car

Al Zubarah is 105 km from Doha, reached via Al Shamal Road heading north — a drive of approximately 1 hour 30 minutes. The route is clearly signposted. No public transport.

Road Trip

Combine Al Zubarah with a stop at Fuwairit Beach, Al Thakhira Mangroves (Al Khor), and Purple Island for a spectacular full-day northern Qatar road trip.

Guided Tours

Several Doha-based tour operators offer guided day-trips to Al Zubarah including the fort, ruins, and surrounding beaches. Recommended for first-time visitors.
