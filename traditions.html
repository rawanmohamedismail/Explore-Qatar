Culture & Heritage

Traditions of Qatar

Discover the rich tapestry of customs, arts, and social traditions that define Qatari identity.

Living Heritage

Qatari Traditions Today

Qatar's traditions are deeply rooted in Bedouin culture and Islamic values. Despite rapid modernisation, the Qatari people have made a concerted effort to preserve and celebrate their heritage, passing it on to future generations through education, festivals, and daily practice.

Hospitality is the cornerstone of Qatari culture. Guests are always welcomed with qahwa (Arabic coffee) and dates, and the generous spirit of the Qatari host is legendary throughout the Arab world.

Cultural Traditions

Living Heritage of Qatar

Experience the traditions that have shaped Qatari society across the centuries.

Art

Arabic Calligraphy

Considered the highest form of visual art in Islamic culture, Arabic calligraphy adorns mosques, public spaces, and artwork throughout Qatar.

Music

Traditional Music

The Ardha — a traditional sword dance — and Leiwah music represent Qatar's rich oral and musical heritage, performed at national celebrations.

Sport

Falconry

Falconry is not merely a sport but a profound cultural tradition. Qatar hosts world-class falconry championships and maintains dedicated centres for the ancient art.

Dress

Traditional Dress

The thobe for men and abaya for women remain the everyday dress of many Qataris, representing modesty, identity, and cultural pride.

Celebration

National Day

Celebrated on 18 December, Qatar's National Day is the most important occasion of the year, marked by parades, fireworks, and cultural performances.

Hospitality

Qahwa & Dates

The serving of Arabic coffee and dates is a sacred ritual of hospitality that no guest leaves without experiencing in a Qatari home.

Immerse Yourself in Qatari Culture

Participate in cultural events, visit heritage sites, and experience the warmth of Qatari hospitality.
